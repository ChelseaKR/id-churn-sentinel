# Go-to-market and business model

## Positioning

**For organizations that maintain transgender identity-document guidance, ID Churn Sentinel is a proposed multi-jurisdiction freshness and governance layer that identifies which registry-claimed source deserves human attention. It aims to combine dated source verification and fetch eligibility, text evidence across heterogeneous surfaces, independent review/correction, explicit gaps/run health, and a no-reader-tracking public contract. It never interprets law.**

The honest novelty claim is narrow: the dated scan in [`16-RESEARCH-SOURCES.md`](./16-RESEARCH-SOURCES.md) found guides, filing tools, generic monitors, and Namesake's daily canonical-source PDF extracted-text/line-diff workflow, but no purpose-built US trans-ID offering publicly documenting the entire combined contract above. Namesake is direct evidence that PDF freshness itself is not whitespace. Run the reproducible refresh at each gate; this is a combined-contract hypothesis, so do not claim “no competitors” or imply that prospective partners lack monitors.

## Segments and wedge

| Segment | Acute job | Buying motion | V1 offer |
|---|---|---|---|
| National advocacy content teams | prioritize updates across jurisdictions | partnership / grant-funded operations | design-partner feed mapping and workflow integration |
| State legal-aid/name-change clinics | know when local source needs review | small annual subscription or sponsor | per-jurisdiction RSS plus response support |
| Document-assistance platforms | keep structured workflows from silently aging | engineering/product procurement | versioned JSON, mapping, SLA, evidence export |
| Researchers/journalists | longitudinal primary-source record | open public good / project sponsorship | public archive and cited releases |

Start with two high-trust design partners, not broad direct-to-consumer acquisition. Do not market to individuals as a legal guidance product.

## Product packaging

### Open public layer

Public aggregate and jurisdiction feeds, sources/gaps/status, schemas, documentation, correction path, and core code remain open. This preserves access, auditability, and the privacy benefit of anonymous consumption.

### Paid institutional service

- source-to-content mapping workshop;
- private routing to the institution’s ticket/Slack system without sharing constituent data;
- operational SLA and named support;
- custom jurisdiction/document-class onboarding that meets common evidence rules;
- evidence exports and quarterly workflow/freshness review;
- training for partner reviewers.

Payment buys service and integration, never suppression, classification influence, access to reader data, or a private “truer” factual feed.

## Pricing hypotheses to test

- Pilot: free or grant-underwritten for six weeks, with staff time and success criteria committed by both sides.
- Small clinic: $1,500–$5,000/year sponsored tier for mapping/support, while RSS remains free.
- National nonprofit/platform: $12,000–$30,000/year for onboarding, SLA, integrations, and quarterly reviews.
- Foundation/public-interest sponsor: $75,000–$150,000/year to fund common operations and community governance with no editorial control.

These are interview hypotheses, not established willingness to pay. Quote only after understanding procurement, budget owner, and public-good expectations.

## Sales and partnership process

1. Lead with a partner’s recent stale-source workflow, not a generic demo.
2. Map 10–20 of its pages to candidate sources and show limitations/gaps first.
3. Run the six-week shadow pilot without replacing existing legal review.
4. Deliver a closeout: time saved, usable alerts, misses, false drift, safety incidents, integration cost, and sustainable operating proposal.
5. Convert only if the feed enters a standing workflow and the partner assigns an owner.

Target outreach: A4TE, Trans Lifeline, Namesake, state legal-aid/name-change clinics, law-school clinics, and foundations funding trans legal infrastructure. Obtain consent before naming any organization as a partner.

## Funnel and economics

Track qualified workflow conversations → pilot LOIs → completed pilots → standing integrations → paid renewals. V1 targets 8 discovery conversations, 2 pilots, 1 standing integration, and 1 payer or committed funding path. Track human review cost per active source and gross margin on paid services, but never optimize away community review.

Initial cost model includes founder/engineering time, paid verification and community panel, specialist legal/security/accessibility review, hosting/domain/backups, and partner support. The public feed is cheap technically; trustworthy human operations are the primary cost and the defensible moat.

## Competitive and substitute response

- **Generic monitors:** cheaper and flexible, but partner must build source registry, noise handling, evidence, review, and jurisdiction contract.
- **Existing guides:** trusted complements and customers, not targets to displace.
- **Filing tools:** may consume the feed; they solve execution after interpretation.
- **Namesake's PDF monitor:** a material domain substitute for canonical-source PDFs, already providing daily extracted-text comparison, changed-line diffs, and issue workflow. Treat it as reusable infrastructure and a partnership/build comparator.
- **Manual research:** remains necessary for legal meaning; the product narrows where to spend it.

If a partner’s generic monitor and existing review process match the pilot outcomes at lower total cost, recommend it. That finding invalidates the commercial wedge, not the need for honest freshness.

### Build / partner / buy gate

Before freezing the PDF or orchestration architecture, compare three options with dated evidence:

1. **Partner/reuse first:** test Namesake's monitor against the V1 PDF corpus; assess license compatibility, extraction quality, exclusion governance, issue/output contract, accessibility of evidence, maintenance ownership, and an upstream contribution path.
2. **Buy/configure:** test whether a generic monitor plus the project's registry/governance layer meets the same safety, privacy, run-health, correction, and partner-denominator gates at lower total cost.
3. **Build only the delta:** retain custom code only where neither option meets the heterogeneous-source, shared eligibility, independent-publication, public-feed, or evidence-retention requirements.

Record the decision, rejected alternatives, total cost, security/privacy implications, and migration/exit path at scope lock. A superior reuse, partnership, or buy result changes the architecture; it is not a threat to be rationalized away.

## Brand and trust rules

Never use fear-based acquisition, expose hostile-policy readership, imply endorsement, publish partner performance rankings, or describe the product as protecting users from law. Say exactly what was observed and tested. Publicly disclose sponsors, conflicts, limitations, corrections, service health, and governance.
